A Typeface Designed by

Jeremey Tribby

Jeremy Tribby is a Californian artist, designer, and technologist. He is the creator of the Arthouse family of typefaces for NBC Universal, which is featured on the web and in broadcast by NBC and Telemundo properties worldwide.

Barlow is a slightly rounded, low-contrast, grotesque type family. Drawing from the visual style of the California public, Barlow shares qualities with the state's car plates, highway signs, buses, and trains. This is the Normal family, which is part of the superfamily along with Semi Condensed and Condensed, each with 9 weights in Roman and Italic.

Barlow is a typeface that was created in 2007 in San Francisco. Tribby was an engineer and designer for the nonprofit Electronic Frontier Foundation, an organization that advocates for the preservation and protection of individual freedoms on the internet.
